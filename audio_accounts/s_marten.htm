Conscientious objector:
Howard Cruttenden Marten
 

On 'field punishment' at the Cinder City Camp, Le Havre.

Transcript: In its most extreme form, a man can be tied up to a gun carriage, which isn't at all a pleasant thing, but normally he is sent to what is known as a Field Punishment Barracks and there the prisoners are tied up for three nights out of four - tied up maybe to a fence or to ropes with their arms extended and their feet tied together or they are maybe tied back to back, it varies in form, and that's done for two hours. Well, it's not exactly a pleasant experience.
